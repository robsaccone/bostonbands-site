The Story

30 Years ofLocal Music

BostonBands.com was founded in 1996 by Alex Willis with a simple mission: to connect local music fans with the bands and music they love. For 30 years, we provided this service for free to Boston-area musicians, venues, and fans of live music and good times.

What started as a simple directory grew into the largest online community for New England local music—a place where 13,000+ bands, 6,700+ venues, and thousands of fans came together around the music they loved. From basement shows in Allston to stages at the Paradise and Middle East, if it was local and it rocked, it was on BostonBands.

Through the Years
1996
The Beginning

Before Google, before MySpace, before anyone knew what a "dot com" could be—BostonBands staked its claim on the early internet. The original site had a graffiti wall, a visitor counter, and a "Best experienced with Internet Explorer" badge. It was raw, it was weird, and it was exactly right for the time. By 2001 came the "Site of the Second Revolution" era.

2008
Growing the Community

The Facebook era arrived, but Boston's music scene already had its own social network. BostonBands grew into a true community—band directories, gig calendars, forums, classifieds, and T-shirt sales. Bands managed their own profiles and fans argued about the best Tuesday night shows. By 2012, the "WICKED MUSIC" redesign arrived just in time for people to check gig listings on their BlackBerrys. Thousands of bands and over 5,000 venues already called it home.

2016
20th Anniversary Relaunch

Twenty years in, BostonBands got its biggest reinvention. The dark theme and the iconic fist logo gave the site a proper identity, and BostonBands Radio let you stream original tracks from local artists while you figured out where to go that night. Smartphones meant you could find a show from the bar you were already at. At its peak: 13,500+ bands, 6,700+ venues, 4,300+ songs, and over 36,000 gig listings.

The Archive

Thirty years after that first graffiti wall went live, the curtain comes down—but the music doesn't disappear. This site is the archive, a place to preserve what BostonBands built: the bands, the venues, the songs, and the memories. Some things are worth keeping.
